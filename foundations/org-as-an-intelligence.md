# The org-as-an-intelligence contract

The second contract. The **ontology contract** (`ontology.md`) says how the model is *structured*; **this one says how the subject is *organized*** — as an **intelligence**. Grounded in **Dorsey & Botha, *"From Hierarchy to Intelligence"*** (Block + Sequoia, 2026); the vocabulary is used as they define it. `will.md` sits above this.

## The thesis
A company can be built **as an intelligence** ("a mini-AGI"): the intelligence lives in the **system**; the **people are on the edge**. What used to flow through layers of management now flows through the system — *"there is no need for a permanent middle-management layer."*

## The five things you build *(cited elsewhere as §1–§5, by item number)*
1. **Company world model** — *"how the company understands itself and its own operations, performance, and priorities, replacing the information that used to flow through layers of management."* Built from recorded, machine-readable artifacts — *"every single thing that we do creates some sort of artifact, whether it be a Slack message, an email, pull request, code, Google document, a meeting that we record"* (podcast). → `world-model/company/`.
2. **Customer world model** — the per-customer understanding of what the subject *serves*, built from its own transactions, that **compounds** as interactions accrete: *"The richer the signal, the better the model. The better the model, the more transactions. The more transactions, the richer the signal."* → `world-model/customer/` (and, in the analysis, the `CustomerGraph` — `../bending-spoons-as-an-intelligence.md §3`). Where the signal is unjoined, that is the **gap**.
3. **Capabilities** — *"atomic … building blocks that are hard to acquire and maintain … not products … they have no UIs of their own,"* held to reliability / compliance / performance targets only. → `capabilities/` (and, in the analysis, the `Capability` object: products unbundled — `../bending-spoons-as-an-intelligence.md §3`). A capability is **invocable** — composed on demand, not merely executed — and **the invoker migrates**. In the hierarchy / machine (today) capabilities are invoked by the **operator**: the acquisition-and-transformation levers (source · screen · underwrite · close · transform · hire), and the customer sits *outside* the loop — a sum of silos whose signal leaks. In the intelligence (the destination) they are invoked by the **customer**, composed at the edge; and by invoking them the customer feeds signal that enriches the customer world model (item 2), so **the customer becomes a contributor to the intelligence, not merely its recipient** — crossing from outside the org to inside it. The source's own move is broader — the org itself going from management layers to a system (§2 above). *Our reading instantiates that move at the capability level as this migration of the invoker, operator → customer* `[thesis]`. That is why the model's capabilities today are operator verbs, while the analysis's `Capability` object is the customer-composable product primitives. **Scope note (do not conflate):** the repo's `capabilities/` folder — and the `list_capabilities` tool — hold the ontology's **kinetic contracts**: the *actions* (write objects) and *functions* (read them), `../ontology.md §2`. A Dorsey **capability** is the *composable, invocable competency*; it is **realized by** those contracts, not identical to them. Same machinery, two grains: the competency (capability) vs the kinetic contract (action / function) that implements it.
4. **Intelligence layer** — *"composes capabilities into solutions for specific customers at specific moments and delivers them proactively."* → the runtime that composes (in the analysis: `orchestrate-capabilities`, `../bending-spoons-as-an-intelligence.md §4`).
5. **Interfaces / the edge** — *"delivery surfaces through which the intelligence layer delivers composed solutions"* (Square, Cash App, TIDAL…). The **edge** is *"where the intelligence makes contact with reality. People reach into places the model can't go yet. They sense things the model can't perceive: intuition, opinionated direction, cultural context, trust dynamics, the feeling in a room."* → `interfaces/` (today the surfaces ARE the products, `interfaces/`; in the analysis they become interchangeable, plus the `meta-interface` that composes across them — `../bending-spoons-as-an-intelligence.md §3`). **And an interface is not only delivery — it collects.** The source names them *"delivery surfaces"* and locates the human sensing at *"the edge"*; but its own compounding loop — *"the richer the signal, the better the model … the more transactions, the richer the signal"* — means the very surface that delivers a composed solution is where the customer's action becomes **signal**: delivery outward, signal inward. `[thesis]` So the interface is bidirectional, and the collection half is arguably the more important one — it is the **sensing side of the feedback loop** and the concrete way the customer becomes a *contributor* (item 2), not merely a recipient. In Bending Spoons today that collected signal exists but is **siloed per interface** (per-product MAU/retention, no unified account) — the flywheel's leak (`../ontology.md §3`); the analysis's `meta-interface` is what would compose it. **This — a bidirectional delivery-and-collection surface, not Palantir's polymorphic interface — is what our "interface" means.**

## The load-bearing mechanism: composition-failure = the roadmap
*"When the intelligence layer tries to compose a solution and can't because the capability doesn't exist, that failure signal is the future roadmap … customer reality generates the backlog directly."* → in the analysis: the meta-interface's **failure-to-compose is a buy signal** (it steers M&A and capital, `../bending-spoons-as-an-intelligence.md §4`). **But not *directly* — that "directly" is where our `will` enters.** A composition-failure is a *signal*; it is confronted with the will (intent + refusals) before it becomes roadmap. The will — this project's addition, above both source frameworks — is what makes the org **directed, not dragged by its customers**: a signal the will refuses (sell an asset, buy outside the thesis) never becomes roadmap.

## Coordination replaces hierarchy
The world model handles alignment; three roles replace management — **ICs** (deep specialists; the world model gives the context a manager used to), **DRIs** (own a cross-cutting problem, with authority to pull resources), **player-coaches** (build people). The org is a **system**, not a pyramid.

## What this contract requires of the repo
1. Organize the subject as an intelligence: `world-model/` (company + customer) + `capabilities/` + `interfaces/`, **moved by the `will`**.
2. **Two world models** — the company (what it *is*) and the customer (what it *serves*); the customer side is built from the subject's own transactions; where it is unbuilt, name the **gap**.
3. **Capabilities are atomic and UI-less** — a capability does one thing; the UI is an *interface*, never the capability.
4. **Interfaces are the edge / the surfaces** (Dorsey) — not Palantir polymorphism; judgment lives at the edge, with people.
5. **Composition-failure is a *signal*, not the roadmap** — what the intelligence cannot compose is a build-or-buy *candidate*; it becomes roadmap only after it is confronted with the **will** (the intent that gives direction + the refusals that bound it). Without the will an intelligence is a model *dragged by its customers*; the will is what makes it directed — a composition-failure the will refuses never becomes roadmap. (This is our refinement of the source's "directly".)

## the model → the analysis
The model (at the repo root) is the subject as a **hierarchy / machine** (today), and it is the only *model* in the repo. The subject as an **intelligence** (the five things built, the customer graph made central, capabilities composed at the edge) is deliberately **not a second model but a declared analysis**: `../bending-spoons-as-an-intelligence.md`. The move between them *is* "from hierarchy to intelligence": this contract defines the generic destination; the analysis argues the Bending-Spoons instance. A thesis in essay form cannot be mistaken for a fact in model form — that separation is itself part of the discipline.
