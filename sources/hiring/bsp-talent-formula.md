## Facts (attributed to the essay)
- Hiring is treated **as a science** — a data-informed, analytical approach over a large application volume (the essay cites 500,000+ annual applications; the F-1 gives the 2025 figure: ~800,000 → 286, `bsp-f1` ~L284).
- A dedicated **Talent team** — including data scientists, researchers, psychologists, and hiring managers — **reports directly to the CEO**. This independence lets it optimize for the long term rather than any team's short-term need.
- **The hiring decision is made by a role-specific committee, separate from the direct lead** — the manager who will work with the hire does not make the final call. (This is the structural reason the `hire` final decision is a committee gate, matching `bsp-selection-process.md`.)
- **Selection dimensions** (assessed via problem-solving, role-specific, and behavioral tasks):
  1. ability to solve **unfamiliar reasoning problems**,
  2. **domain expertise**,
  3. **motivation, ambition, and ownership**.
- Philosophy: they seek people who **excel on a few key dimensions** rather than well-rounded candidates without peaks — "spiky" talent over uniformly-good.

## How this grounds `hire`
- Reinforces the **committee** as the human gate (decision independent of the direct lead; Talent team under the CEO).
- Gives the three assessment dimensions that the `hire` gates operationalize, consistent with `bsp-selection-process.md` and the F-1 selection values.
