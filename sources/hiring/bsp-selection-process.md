## What they look for (the selection objective)
- Goal: determine whether a candidate can *"become a strong performer at Bending Spoons within a reasonable timeframe."*
- **Long-term potential outweighs current expertise** — they weight *"how quickly a candidate can acquire new skills and adapt"* to a fast-paced, ever-changing environment.
- Design method: before recruiting for a role, they *"identify the most important traits an outstanding candidate would possess,"* then design the process to evaluate those traits. **Stages build on each other.**

## The funnel — five stages
1. **Screening** — the CV: academic records, professional experience, personal pursuits, achievements; quantitative signal encouraged (GPA, graduation grade, GMAT/SAT). Cover letter optional but appreciated.
2. **Tasks** — one to six hours across multiple rounds, sometimes proctored (opt-in). Three types:
   - **Problem-solving** — *"how quickly and effectively you can make sense of unfamiliar problems"* (correlates strongly with success at BS).
   - **Behavioral** — *"ownership, commitment, ambition, and the drive to pursue exceptional results as part of a team."*
   - **Role-specific** — expertise and potential in the role; *"how fast you grow, not where you start."*
3. **Interviews** — typically two or three (problem-solving / behavioral / role-specific); the candidate meets Spooners; two-way. **Honesty gate:** *"we won't extend an offer if we determine that a candidate is misrepresenting accomplishments or overstating contributions."*
4. **Reference check** — references who know the work well (senior colleagues, managers, professors, thesis supervisors).
5. **Final decision** — made by a **hiring committee**, *"to keep arbitrary choices and personal bias out of the process."* Unsuccessful candidates may reapply after at least a year.

## Support (process design, not gates)
- **Candidate support managers** — a dedicated point of contact, usually assigned after the initial stages.
- **Accommodations** — for disability and neurodivergence, at every stage; requesting them does not affect chances.
- **Feedback** — detailed feedback to candidates who reach the final stages; general feedback on request.

## How this grounds the `hire` capability
- The **gates** (what is assessed) are the traits above — problem-solving ability, role expertise + potential,
  and the behavioral traits (ownership · commitment · ambition · drive for exceptional results as a team),
  under an explicit **talent-over-experience** and **honesty** rule. These align with the F-1's selection
  values (talent density, technical excellence, truth-seeking, extreme ownership — `bsp-f1` ~L138/~L158/~L283).
- The **funnel** (screening → tasks → interviews → references → committee) is the process the `hire` agent runs
  a candidate through; the **final decision is a committee**, which is the human gate the engine enforces.
- The 2025 funnel scale is in the filing: ~800,000 applications → 286 hired, <0.04% (`bsp-f1` ~L284).
