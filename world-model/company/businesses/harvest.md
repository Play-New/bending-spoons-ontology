## What it is
Harvest offers time-tracking and invoicing software for freelancers and professional-services firms (bsp-f1 ~L386; bsp-424b4 ~L366). The legal entity acquired is Iridesco, LLC (a New York limited liability company, doing business as Harvest) (bsp-f1 ~L228, ~L4969; bsp-424b4 ~L5073). It is one of Bending Spoons' ten main businesses in Q1 2026 (alphabetically AOL, Brightcove, Eventbrite, Evernote, Harvest, komoot, Remini, StreamYard, Vimeo, and WeTransfer) (bsp-f1 ~L375; bsp-424b4 ~L355). The Harvest word mark and logo are among the material trademark registrations and pending applications Bending Spoons holds (bsp-f1 ~L1183; bsp-424b4 ~L1166). On its own site Harvest positions itself as helping professional teams "track time, bill clients, optimize team time, and measure performance," with time tracking, invoicing and payments, expense management, budget tracking, reporting, and 40+ integrations (site-harvest, https://www.getharvest.com/) — [to-validate — site only, not in the filings].

## The deal
On July 31, 2025 Bending Spoons completed the acquisition of 100% of the issued and outstanding equity securities of Iridesco, LLC (d/b/a Harvest), accounted for as a business combination (bsp-f1 ~L4969-4971; bsp-424b4 ~L5073-5075). Transaction costs (including professional fees) attributable to the Harvest acquisition were $1 million, recognized in general-and-administrative expense (bsp-f1 ~L4972; bsp-424b4 ~L5076). Harvest is one of the six businesses acquired in 2025 (Brightcove, Harvest, komoot, Loomly, MileIQ, and Vimeo) (bsp-f1 ~L1870, ~L2034; bsp-424b4 ~L1860, ~L2024).

No standalone price is disclosed for Harvest. The total consideration paid for the Loomly, komoot, MileIQ, and Harvest acquisitions **combined** was $701 million (fair value of net assets acquired $701,253 thousand) (bsp-f1 ~L4973-4974; bsp-424b4 ~L5077-5078). The purchase-price allocation for these four acquisitions combined recorded $469,086 thousand goodwill, $30,678 thousand intellectual properties, $222,886 thousand customer base, and $32,785 thousand other intangible assets (bsp-f1 ~L4974; bsp-424b4 ~L5078); goodwill acquired in 2025 of $469 million was attributable to the Loomly, komoot, MileIQ, and Harvest acquisitions (bsp-f1 ~L5073; bsp-424b4 ~L5177). None of these figures is broken out for Harvest alone. The fair-value determination of the acquired intangible assets for the Harvest business combination was flagged as a critical audit matter, given the significant judgment involved (including assumptions on Harvest's revenue growth rate) (bsp-f1 ~L4648-4656; bsp-424b4 ~L4751-4759).

Stub-period contribution is likewise reported only in aggregate: the 2025 consolidated income statement includes combined revenue of $119 million and income before tax of $27 million from Loomly, komoot, MileIQ, and Harvest for the periods from their respective acquisition dates (bsp-f1 ~L4977; bsp-424b4 ~L5081). Harvest is also included in the unaudited pro-forma combined results presented as if it had been acquired on January 1, 2024 (alongside komoot, Loomly, MileIQ, Brightcove, and Vimeo) (bsp-f1 ~L5008; bsp-424b4 ~L5112).

## The playbook applied (where the OFFICIAL record shows it)
The filings record that in 2025 Bending Spoons offered separation packages to team members in connection with the reorganizations of Brightcove, Harvest, komoot, Loomly, MileIQ, and WeTransfer — these appear as an expense driver across research-and-development, sales-and-marketing, and general-and-administrative lines (bsp-f1 ~L1883, ~L1887, ~L1891; bsp-424b4 ~L1873, ~L1877, ~L1881), and as the 2025 driver of the reorganization-related expense line (bsp-f1 ~L1951; bsp-424b4 ~L1941). Harvest is named in each of these reorganization lists.

The filings also disclose the staffing mechanic directly: to staff the Harvest team following the July 2025 acquisition, Bending Spoons redeployed approximately half of the team previously dedicated to Evernote, "enabling us to execute the Harvest transformation quickly and effectively" (bsp-f1 ~L2721; bsp-424b4 ~L2713). This is the platform-reallocation move visible for Harvest — a shared talent pool moved off a mature asset (Evernote) onto a fresh one. Any specific headcount or layoff percentage for Harvest is [to-validate — press only]; the filings state the reorganization occurred and describe the Evernote redeploy but disclose no separate Harvest headcount figure.

On the revenue side, the filings state that "Other" revenue includes commissions earned by Harvest in connection with invoice payment facilitation for its customers (bsp-f1 ~L1689; bsp-424b4 ~L1679) — i.e., Harvest monetizes payments processed through its invoicing flow, alongside its subscription revenue.

The product's own site continues to operate under the Harvest brand with an active tiered pricing model — Free ($0, 1 seat, 2 projects), Teams ($9 per seat/month or $108 per seat billed annually), and Enterprise ($14 per seat/month or $168 per seat billed annually), with additional invoices, projects, clients, and tasks billed by usage (site-harvest, https://www.getharvest.com/pricing). ## What holds the customer
The one signal in the official record is the payment-facilitation commission: Harvest earns commissions on invoice payment facilitation for its customers (bsp-f1 ~L1689; bsp-424b4 ~L1679), meaning revenue rises with the customer's own committed billing activity — a paying-user, revenue-critical orientation rather than a free-tool one. The filings do not break out Harvest's MAU, paying-customer count, ARPU, or net revenue retention (Harvest appears only in aggregate deal, reorganization, and combined stub-revenue disclosures), so a precise retention/churn/ARPU read is [not disclosed at the per-business level in the filings]. ## Where it sits in the machine
Harvest is a recently acquired (July 31, 2025) SaaS still inside its post-acquisition reorganization window — the filings show it in the 2025 reorganization/separation-package lists and describe the Evernote-to-Harvest redeploy — so its lifecycle stage is transforming (bsp-f1 ~L1883, ~L2721; bsp-424b4 ~L1873, ~L2713). It is named among the ten Q1 2026 main businesses alongside productivity/creator peers (Evernote, WeTransfer, komoot) and the video cluster (Vimeo, Brightcove) (bsp-f1 ~L375; bsp-424b4 ~L355); the filings list it among the main businesses but assert no formal Harvest-specific cluster.
