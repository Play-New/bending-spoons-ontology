## What it is
WeTransfer enables digital file storage and distribution for individuals and businesses (bsp-f1 ~L396; bsp-424b4 ~L376). It caters to professionals, small enterprises, and their audiences, and in March 2026 had 58 million monthly active users and 1 million monthly paying customers (bsp-f1 ~L373; bsp-424b4 ~L353). Its own site markets it as a service to "Send Large Files Fast" (site-wetransfer, https://wetransfer.com/). The filings characterize it as a digital file management and transfer service used mainly by professionals and organizations, whose usage tends to exhibit the opposite pattern from consumer apps — slowing during summer as collaborative projects pause (bsp-f1 ~L2008; bsp-424b4 ~L1998). It competes with digital file storage and distribution tools such as Google Drive and Smash (bsp-f1 ~L2878; bsp-424b4 ~L2870). Its legal owner is Wetransfer B.V., a private limited liability company under Dutch law (bsp-f1 ~L4945; bsp-424b4 ~L5049).

## The deal
On July 31, 2024, Bending Spoons acquired 100% of the share capital of The Creative Productivity Group B.V. (then the ultimate parent of Wetransfer B.V.) for a total consideration of $476.3 million (bsp-f1 ~L4943; bsp-424b4 ~L5047). Of the $546 million of goodwill acquired in 2024, $334 million was attributable to the WeTransfer acquisition (and $212 million to Meetup, StreamYard, and Issuu) (bsp-f1 ~L5072; bsp-424b4 ~L5176). For the stub period from acquisition to year-end (July 31 to December 31, 2024) WeTransfer contributed $61 million of revenue and a $17 million loss before tax (bsp-f1 ~L4951; bsp-424b4 ~L5055). The 2024 acquisitions (Issuu, Meetup, Mosaic, StreamYard, and WeTransfer) collectively drove the year's revenue growth (bsp-f1 ~L1863; bsp-424b4 ~L1853). In connection with the acquisition, certain unvested equity awards held by WeTransfer team members became subject to accelerated vesting upon the change in control (bsp-f1 ~L4953; bsp-424b4 ~L5057); 2024 transaction-related expense included $5 million of costs related to the acceleration of equity instruments held by the acquired teams at Issuu and WeTransfer (bsp-f1 ~L1946; bsp-424b4 ~L1936).

The monetization side then shows through. Organic revenue growth was 13% in 2025, and WeTransfer was among the largest contributors (with Issuu, Meetup, Remini, StreamYard), the increase driven by higher subscription revenue from a rise in both average revenue per subscriber and number of subscribers, partly offset by a decrease in advertising revenue (bsp-f1 ~L1871, ~L1875; bsp-424b4 ~L1861, ~L1865). The same pattern continued into Q1 2026, where organic revenue growth was 6% and WeTransfer was again a top contributor (with komoot and Meetup) on higher ARPU and subscriber count (bsp-f1 ~L1911, ~L1913; bsp-424b4 ~L1901, ~L1903). WeTransfer is also named among the primary drivers of the company's advertising revenue (with AOL and Remini), which was 12% of revenue in Q1 2026 (bsp-f1 ~L1686; bsp-424b4 ~L1676), and the company sells a portion of its advertising inventory directly to advertisers, primarily on WeTransfer (bsp-f1 ~L2883; bsp-424b4 ~L2875).

## What holds the customer
For WeTransfer the load-bearing signal is willingness-to-pay of the committed professional user. The filings evidence it directly: revenue growth is driven by rising average revenue per subscriber together with a rising number of subscribers, not by advertising (which was declining over the same window) (bsp-f1 ~L1875; bsp-424b4 ~L1865). That combination — more paying users each paying more, even as the free/ad side softens — is the retention-and-monetization tell of a converted committed base. The filings also name WeTransfer's "sender-recipient dynamics" as a brand/network-effect that limits the risk of replacement by general-purpose AI chatbots (grouped with Eventbrite's organizer-attendee dynamics) (bsp-f1 ~L2851; bsp-424b4 ~L2843) — the moat is the two-sided send/receive relationship, not the file-transfer commodity itself. ## Where it sits in the machine
Lifecycle: optimized. WeTransfer is one of the acquired businesses the filings explicitly credit with improved cash generation "following our transformations of these businesses" (alongside Issuu, Meetup, and StreamYard), which lifted net cash from operating activities in 2025 (bsp-f1 ~L2030; bsp-424b4 ~L2020). It was among Bending Spoons' main businesses in Q1 2026 (with AOL, Brightcove, Eventbrite, Evernote, Harvest, komoot, Remini, StreamYard, Vimeo), a group that in aggregate accounted for more than 80% of revenue for the period (bsp-f1 ~L375, ~L1694; bsp-424b4 ~L355, ~L1684). WeTransfer's trademark word marks and logos are among the group's material registered/pending marks (bsp-f1 ~L1183; bsp-424b4 ~L1166). Post-acquisition it was integrated into the group's capital structure: Wetransfer B.V. became a co-borrower (with Bending Spoons US Inc.) on euro-denominated Tranche B term loan commitments of up to €300 million, fully funded on January 30, 2026, whose proceeds financed ongoing M&A (bsp-f1 ~L5433; bsp-424b4 ~L5537). Its portfolio role is a professional/prosumer productivity-and-creative-workflow asset; the filings do not group it into an enterprise-video cluster (that clustering role belongs to Vimeo and Brightcove), so no such clustering is asserted here.
