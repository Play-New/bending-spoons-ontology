## What it is
Loomly is a social media management platform for marketers: the F-1 states plainly, "Loomly offers social media management tools for marketers" (bsp-f1 ~L4958; bsp-424b4 ~L5062), and lists Loomly Holdco, Inc. among the businesses acquired in 2025 (bsp-f1 ~L228; bsp-424b4 ~L206). Its own site describes it as "Stress-free social media management" that spans "From content ideas and post creation to scheduling and engagement. For all your channels, all powered by one seamless AI system" (site-loomly, https://www.loomly.com/). The audience is modern marketing teams and brands managing multiple social channels, with collaboration and approval workflows (site-loomly, https://www.loomly.com/). Stated capabilities include AI post/image/caption generation, 10+ channels covered from a calendar, 24/7 AI-powered engagement for comments and DMs, and social listening to track sentiment and brand mentions (site-loomly, https://www.loomly.com/).

## The deal
On January 1, 2025, Bending Spoons completed the acquisition of 100% of the issued and outstanding equity securities of Loomly Holdco, Inc., a Delaware corporation and the owner of Loomly, accounting for it as a business combination (bsp-f1 ~L4957; bsp-424b4 ~L5061). The F-1 characterizes the product tersely: "Loomly offers social media management tools for marketers" (bsp-f1 ~L4958; bsp-424b4 ~L5062). Transaction costs (including professional fees) amounted to $0.4 million, recognized in general and administrative expense (bsp-f1 ~L4960). The F-1 does not disclose a standalone price for Loomly: instead it reports that "The total consideration paid for the Loomly, komoot, MileIQ, and Harvest acquisitions was $701 million" as a group, so the individual Loomly figure is not disclosed (bsp-f1 ~L4973; bsp-424b4 ~L5077).

The purchase-price allocation is likewise reported only for the four-acquisition group, not for Loomly alone: goodwill of $469,086 thousand, intellectual properties of $30,678 thousand, customer base of $222,886 thousand, other intangible assets of $32,785 thousand, cash and cash equivalents of $46,359 thousand, total assets acquired of $815,737 thousand, total liabilities assumed of $114,484 thousand, and fair value of net assets acquired of $701,253 thousand (bsp-f1 ~L4973). Goodwill acquired in 2025 of $469 million was attributable to the acquisitions of Loomly, komoot, MileIQ, and Harvest (bsp-f1 ~L5073; bsp-424b4 ~L5177). The estimated useful life of the acquired identifiable intangibles is 5 years for intellectual properties, 7 to 14 years for the customer base, and 5 to 10 years for other intangible assets (mainly trademarks) — again on the grouped basis (bsp-f1 ~L4973). ## The playbook applied (where the OFFICIAL record shows it)
The F-1 places Loomly squarely inside the standard playbook. In 2025, research and development, sales and marketing, and general and administrative expense each carried "Separation packages offered to team members in connection with the reorganizations of Brightcove, Harvest, komoot, Loomly, MileIQ, and WeTransfer" (bsp-f1 ~L1883, ~L1887, ~L1891; bsp-424b4 ~L1873, ~L1877, ~L1881) — the reorganization/headcount step applied to Loomly the same year it was bought; the same list drives the full-year reorganization-related expense line (bsp-f1 ~L1951). Q1 2025 reorganization-related expense was likewise "driven by separation packages offered to team members in connection with the reorganizations of Brightcove, Evernote, Issuu, komoot, Loomly, Meetup, StreamYard, and WeTransfer" (bsp-f1 ~L1963; bsp-424b4 ~L1953). The optimization then shows up in cash: net cash from operating activities grew by $54 million, or 254%, from Q1 2025 to Q1 2026, "primarily driven by improved cash generation at Brightcove, Issuu, komoot, Loomly, StreamYard, and WeTransfer following our transformations of these businesses" (bsp-f1 ~L2039; bsp-424b4 ~L2032). The reorganization was already underway in the quarter of purchase: "In Q1 2025, we acquired Brightcove, komoot, and Loomly" (bsp-f1 ~L2041; bsp-424b4 ~L2034). The exact headcount reduction / layoff percentage for Loomly is [to-validate] — press-only; the F-1 states only that separation packages were paid, not how many people. On the product side, the live site shows the current monetized state: an AI-first repositioning with three tiers — Starter at $49/mo billed annually ($65 monthly; 12 social accounts, 3 users), Beyond at $249/mo billed annually ($332 monthly; 60 social accounts, unlimited users), and a custom Enterprise tier for 61+ accounts (site-loomly, https://www.loomly.com/pricing). AI post generation is metered by plan (1/month on Starter, 4/month on Beyond) and "Social listening" and "Reply with AI" are gated to the higher tier (site-loomly, https://www.loomly.com/pricing).

## What holds the customer
For Loomly the load-bearing signal is willingness-to-pay and retention of the committed marketing team, not the trial-taker. The pricing page's structure reveals where that lives: a low-friction 7-day trial with no credit card pulls users in (site-loomly, https://www.loomly.com/), but the revenue is in the Beyond tier — $249/mo annually, unlimited users, 60 accounts, plus social listening and AI reply — aimed at agencies and multi-brand teams whose whole workflow sits inside the tool (site-loomly, https://www.loomly.com/pricing). The F-1 gives an indirect retention/quality read: it reports "improved cash generation at ... Loomly ... following our transformations of these businesses" (bsp-f1 ~L2039; bsp-424b4 ~L2032), meaning post-reorg the business threw off more cash — consistent with a retained, paying base rather than a collapsing one. ## Where it sits in the machine
Loomly is at the optimized end of the lifecycle: acquired Jan 1 2025, reorganized within the same year (and same quarter), and cited by the F-1 among the businesses whose "transformations" lifted Q1 2026 operating cash (bsp-f1 ~L2039). Its portfolio role is cash generation from a mature SaaS niche — social media management for marketers (bsp-f1 ~L4958). Officially it clusters with komoot, MileIQ, and Harvest as one acquisition cohort for accounting purposes: their combined $701 million consideration (bsp-f1 ~L4973) and combined revenue of $119 million with income before tax of $27 million for the periods from the respective acquisition dates are reported as a group (bsp-f1 ~L4977; bsp-424b4 ~L5081). Loomly, being acquired on January 1, 2025, contributed a full 12 months to that 2025 group revenue (vs. partial-year periods for the later-acquired komoot, MileIQ, and Harvest), though the F-1 does not split its share out. Loomly also appears in the supplemental unaudited pro forma information, which presents the combined historical results of Bending Spoons, komoot, Loomly, MileIQ, Harvest, Brightcove, and Vimeo as if acquired on January 1, 2024 (pro forma group revenue $1,761,916 thousand and net income $9,143 thousand for 2025) (bsp-f1 ~L5008; bsp-424b4 ~L5112).
