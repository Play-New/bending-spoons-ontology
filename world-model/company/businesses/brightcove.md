## What it is
Brightcove provides video hosting, management, and streaming solutions for enterprises (bsp-f1 ~L380). It focuses on large enterprises, and in March 2026 had approximately 15,000 monthly active users and 1,700 monthly paying customers (bsp-f1 ~L374) — a small, high-value base relative to consumer businesses in the portfolio (WeTransfer, by contrast, had 58 million MAU and 1 million paying customers in the same period, bsp-f1 ~L373). The owning legal entity is Brightcove Inc., a Delaware corporation; as of December 31, 2025 the group's subsidiaries include both Brightcove Inc. and Brightcove Holdings, Inc. (bsp-f1 ~L4903, ~L4981). Beyond subscriptions, Brightcove also generates other revenue from fees for overages and software integration services (bsp-f1 ~L1689). Its own site describes a comprehensive video platform spanning hosting and live/on-demand streaming, content management, analytics, monetization, OTT app building, and an AI feature suite (site-brightcove, https://www.brightcove.com) — [unofficial — company site, not in bsp-f1/bsp-424b4]. It sells to enterprise segments including broadcasters, media and entertainment publishers, financial services, healthcare, retail, and technology companies (site-brightcove) — [unofficial — company site, not in bsp-f1/bsp-424b4].

## The deal
On February 4, 2025, Bending Spoons acquired 100% of the issued and outstanding equity securities of Brightcove Inc., a Delaware corporation and the owner of Brightcove, for a total consideration of $218 million (bsp-f1 ~L4978). Transaction costs, including professional fees, amounted to $1 million and were recognized in general and administrative expense (bsp-f1 ~L4983). Brightcove contributed revenue of $163 million and a loss before tax of $23 million for the period from the acquisition date (February 4 to December 31, 2025) (bsp-f1 ~L4989).

Purchase price allocation. The fair value of net assets acquired was $217.798 million (bsp-f1 ~L4984), against total consideration of $218 million. The allocation recognized: goodwill of $158.947 million; intellectual properties of $13.977 million; customer base of $74.601 million; other intangible assets (mainly trademarks) of $18.047 million; deferred tax assets of $3.092 million; other non-current assets of $30.022 million; cash and cash equivalents of $21.671 million; and trade receivables and other current assets of $39.109 million — total assets acquired $359.465 million. Liabilities assumed totaled $141.667 million (deferred tax liabilities $28.471 million, other non-current liabilities $17.428 million, accrued and other current liabilities $95.768 million) (bsp-f1 ~L4984). The estimated useful life for the intellectual properties, customer base, and other intangible assets is 5 years, valued using income approaches (bsp-f1 ~L4984, ~L4985). The Brightcove goodwill of $159 million is separately confirmed in the goodwill roll-forward note (bsp-f1 ~L5073).

Equity acceleration. In connection with the acquisition, certain unvested equity awards held by Brightcove team members became subject to accelerated vesting upon the change in control. Under ASC 805, the portion attributable to post-combination vesting — $14 million — was treated as a separate transaction and recognized as compensation costs across cost of revenue, R&D, sales and marketing, and G&A in 2025 (bsp-f1 ~L4986, ~L4988). Q1 2025 transaction-related expense included $14 million of costs related to the acceleration of equity instruments held by the acquired Brightcove team (bsp-f1 ~L1960); for full-year 2025, these acceleration costs are reported jointly for Brightcove and Vimeo ($36 million combined) (bsp-f1 ~L1947).

## The playbook applied (where the OFFICIAL record shows it)
The F-1 places Brightcove squarely inside the acquire-and-reorganize playbook. In 2025, research and development, sales and marketing, and general and administrative expense each included separation packages offered to team members in connection with the reorganizations of Brightcove (among Harvest, komoot, Loomly, MileIQ, and WeTransfer) (bsp-f1 ~L1883, ~L1887, ~L1891). The same reorganizations drove full-year 2025 reorganization-related expense (bsp-f1 ~L1951), and Brightcove was already among the businesses being reorganized in Q1 2025 (bsp-f1 ~L1963); by Q1 2026, Brightcove again appears in the reorganization-expense list alongside AOL, Eventbrite, Vimeo, and WeTransfer (bsp-f1 ~L1964), indicating the reorganization continued into 2026. Those same expense lines also carried costs related to the acceleration of equity instruments held by the acquired teams at Brightcove and Vimeo (bsp-f1 ~L1883, ~L1887, ~L1891). By Q1 2026 the reorganization had already produced cost reductions: R&D, sales-and-marketing, and G&A expense growth was each partly offset by a reduction in the personnel costs associated with the ongoing operations of Brightcove, komoot, and WeTransfer after their reorganizations (bsp-f1 ~L1917, ~L1919, ~L1922) — indicating Brightcove's reorganization was substantially executed within roughly a year of acquisition. The cost discipline also showed in cash: Q1 2026 net cash from operating activities grew 254%, driven partly by improved cash generation at Brightcove (among Issuu, komoot, Loomly, StreamYard, and WeTransfer) following their transformations (bsp-f1 ~L2039). On the product side, the current site shows continued investment in packaged enterprise bundles and an AI feature suite (site-brightcove, https://www.brightcove.com) — [unofficial — company site, not in bsp-f1/bsp-424b4]. The F-1 does not disclose a specific headcount or layoff percentage for Brightcove; any such figure exists only in press — [to-validate — press only].

## What holds the customer
For Brightcove the load-bearing signal is willingness-to-pay and retention within a committed enterprise base — and here the official record is candid about pressure. The F-1 states that overall portfolio growth was partly offset by a decrease in Brightcove revenue caused by a decline in the number of subscribers (bsp-f1 ~L1876). Its customer acquisition is driven primarily by sales efforts rather than organic word of mouth (bsp-f1 ~L431), so the small base of ~1,700 monthly paying customers against ~15,000 monthly active users (bsp-f1 ~L374) makes each account's retention and expansion decisive. The narrow paying-to-active ratio and the disclosed subscriber decline suggest the decisive test for this asset is defending net revenue in the installed enterprise base (offsetting subscriber loss with higher revenue per customer) rather than adding logos — consistent with an optimized, cost-reduced business. This inference goes beyond what the F-1 states: the filing discloses the subscriber decline and the user counts but does not report a Brightcove NRR, ARPU, or revenue-per-customer figure, so the retention-vs-logos framing is analysis, not a stated metric — [thesis, not in filings].

## Where it sits in the machine
Brightcove is at the optimized stage of the lifecycle: acquired February 2025, reorganized within the year, and by Q1 2026 already showing the personnel-cost reductions and improved cash generation that follow a completed reorganization (bsp-f1 ~L1917, ~L2039). It was one of the ten main businesses that in Q1 2026 together accounted for more than 80% of Bending Spoons' revenue (bsp-f1 ~L375, ~L376). The portfolio contains a second enterprise-relevant video asset, Vimeo (acquired November 24, 2025 for $1.36 billion, bsp-f1 ~L4990–L4991), repeatedly grouped with Brightcove in the F-1's equity-acceleration ($36 million combined, bsp-f1 ~L1947), reorganization (bsp-f1 ~L1964), and pro-forma disclosures — the supplemental pro-forma statement presents Brightcove and Vimeo (among komoot, Loomly, MileIQ, Harvest) as if acquired on January 1, 2024 (bsp-f1 ~L5008). The F-1 does not, however, state that the two are operated as a single combined enterprise-video unit, so any such clustering beyond co-mention is [thesis, not in filings]. Brightcove's role is a mature, sales-led B2B cash asset to be run efficiently rather than grown organically (bsp-f1 ~L431, ~L1876).
