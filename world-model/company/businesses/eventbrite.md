## What it is
Eventbrite delivers event creation, ticketing, and discovery services for organizers and attendees (bsp-f1 ~L382; bsp-424b4 ~L362). Its own site describes it as "an all-in-one ticketing and marketing platform" and "a global event ticketing and marketing platform, powering millions of events in nearly 180 countries" (site-eventbrite, https://www.eventbrite.com/l/sell-tickets/). It serves organizers across music, corporate, nonprofit, food & drink, performing arts, and fitness categories, and offers a consumer discovery app where, per its own marketing, event-goers account for roughly 1 in 4 tickets sold (site-eventbrite, https://www.eventbrite.com/l/sell-tickets/). Its trademark word marks and logos are among the brand protections Bending Spoons holds (bsp-f1 ~L1183; bsp-424b4 ~L1166).

## The deal
On March 10, 2026, Bending Spoons acquired 100% of the issued and outstanding equity of Eventbrite, Inc. for a **total consideration of $505 million** — $3.4 million of it equity awards granted in connection with the transaction, the remainder all cash-settled at closing (bsp-f1 ~L4400). The filings carry a full **preliminary purchase-price allocation** (ASC 805): Goodwill $293.3M, Customer base $223.0M, Intellectual properties/technology $29.1M, and other intangible assets (trademark) $47.8M, against **$504.6M total consideration** (bsp-f1 ~L3207, ~L4405). (A second preliminary allocation in the notes shows Goodwill $291.2M against the same $504.6M total, bsp-f1 ~L5394; the allocation is explicitly preliminary and subject to change.) The acquisition being "not significant" for purposes of Rule 3-05 of Regulation S-X (bsp-f1 ~L232; bsp-424b4 ~L211) means only that Bending Spoons did not file Eventbrite's *separate audited historical* financial statements — it does **not** mean the price or PPA are undisclosed. Eventbrite's income statement was also folded into the unaudited pro forma condensed combined income statement for FY2025 "on a voluntary basis" (bsp-424b4 ~L3139), one of the three acquisitions (with AOL Holdco I LLC and Vimeo, Inc.) whose effect the pro forma statements illustrate as if completed on January 1, 2025 (bsp-f1 ~L238; bsp-424b4 ~L218). The transaction drew regulatory attention: in April 2026 Bending Spoons received questions from the U.S. Treasury Department unit that reviews foreign investment transactions not voluntarily filed with CFIUS, in connection with the Eventbrite acquisition; Bending Spoons responded and noted Treasury may request a formal voluntary CFIUS filing (bsp-f1 ~L957–959; bsp-424b4 ~L938–940). Eventbrite is one of three recently acquired businesses — alongside AOL and Vimeo — that both filings single out as the current focus of transformation (bsp-f1 ~L135; bsp-424b4 ~L112).

## The playbook applied (where the OFFICIAL record shows it)
The filings show the reorganization mechanism directly: in Q1 2026, separation packages were offered to team members "in connection with the reorganizations of AOL, Eventbrite, and Vimeo," and this cost is named as a driver of all three operating-expense lines — research and development (up $51M / 116% YoY), sales and marketing (up $21M / 57%), and general and administrative (up $46M / 52%) (bsp-424b4 ~L1907, ~L1909, ~L1912; bsp-f1 ~L1917). The filings also record $5 million of costs related to the acceleration of equity instruments held by the acquired team at Eventbrite in Q1 2026 — the only Eventbrite-specific transaction-related figure disclosed (bsp-f1 ~L1961; bsp-424b4 ~L1951). The specific headcount or layoff percentage for Eventbrite is not stated in either filing [to-validate — press only]; the filings give only the qualitative "separation packages" and "reorganization" language, and no per-product reduction figure. On the product side, Eventbrite's own site evidences an active, packaged offering: free events publish at no cost; paid events carry a "3.7% + $1.79 service fee per ticket" plus a "2.9% payment processing fee per order"; and there is an Eventbrite Pro email-marketing add-on starting at $15/month (site-eventbrite, https://www.eventbrite.com/organizer/pricing/) — these specific rates are NOT in the official filings and are unofficial site figures [to-validate — press only]. The filings corroborate only the qualitative structure: for ticketing services Bending Spoons "earn[s] a fee that's partly fixed and partly based on the value of the ticket sold" (bsp-f1 ~L4306; bsp-424b4 ~L4414), consistent with the site's fixed-plus-percentage take rate. The site also markets "AI-powered tools," Eventbrite Ads, and a discovery app (site-eventbrite, https://www.eventbrite.com/l/sell-tickets/); whether these features post-date the acquisition cannot be confirmed from official sources [to-validate — press only].

## What holds the customer
Eventbrite is the portfolio's structural outlier on monetization, and both filings make this explicit: subscriptions were 84% of total revenue in Q1 2026 and the primary revenue source for all Bending Spoons businesses in the period "with the exception of Eventbrite" (bsp-f1 ~L1682; bsp-424b4 ~L1672). Instead, Eventbrite monetizes through "Eventbrite's share of ticket sales conducted through its systems," and it was the primary driver of the "Other" revenue category (bsp-f1 ~L1689; bsp-424b4 ~L1679). That "Other" category represented 4% of revenue in Q1 2026, "driven primarily by Eventbrite," up from less than 1% in 2023–2024 and 2% in 2025 (bsp-f1 ~L1690–1692; bsp-424b4 ~L1680) — i.e., the group-level "Other" line was essentially created by the Eventbrite acquisition (in absolute terms, Other revenue jumped from $3.0M in Q1 2025 to $23.9M in Q1 2026) (bsp-f1 ~L4325). The revenue-recognition note details the two customer options: where Bending Spoons is the merchant of record it is the principal and recognizes ticketing revenue gross (payment-processing cost booked to cost of revenue); where the organizer uses a third-party processor, Bending Spoons acts as agent and recognizes revenue net (bsp-f1 ~L4310–4320; bsp-424b4 ~L4413–onward). What holds the customer here is therefore not subscription retention but transaction throughput — the willingness of organizers to keep running paid events through Eventbrite's rails at the stated take rate (fee "partly fixed and partly based on the value of the ticket sold," bsp-f1 ~L4306). A committed organizer who lists paid events repeatedly is the retained, revenue-generating user; the free-events tier is top-of-funnel that does not itself pay (site-eventbrite, https://www.eventbrite.com/organizer/pricing/ — unofficial). Net revenue retention, the group's retention metric, is a subscription measure and is not applicable to Eventbrite: the filings disclose per-business NRR only for AOL, Evernote, Remini, and StreamYard, and Eventbrite is expressly the non-subscription exception (bsp-f1 ~L1702–1703; bsp-424b4). No Eventbrite-specific MAU, paying-customer, churn, or ARPU figure is broken out in either filing [to-validate — press only].

## Where it sits in the machine
Eventbrite is at the transforming stage of the lifecycle: acquired March 2026 and named in both filings as one of the three businesses (with AOL and Vimeo) where "much of our current focus" on transformation now sits (bsp-f1 ~L135; bsp-424b4 ~L112). It is one of the ten main businesses (alphabetically AOL, Brightcove, Eventbrite, Evernote, Harvest, komoot, Remini, StreamYard, Vimeo, WeTransfer) that in aggregate accounted for more than 80% of Bending Spoons revenue in Q1 2026 (bsp-f1 ~L375, ~L381–382; bsp-424b4 ~L355, ~L361–362). The acquisition fits the stated capital-allocation philosophy of "favoring returns over organic revenue growth": a target's projected organic growth is embedded in Bending Spoons' assumptions and influences the price it will pay, but the investment decision is made on expected returns "regardless of the organic growth profile and assuming we'll never sell the acquired business" (bsp-f1 ~L162–164; bsp-424b4 ~L139). Eventbrite's word marks and logos are among the trademark registrations and applications Bending Spoons holds across the U.S., E.U., and other jurisdictions (bsp-f1 ~L1183; bsp-424b4 ~L1166). It does not cluster into an obvious sub-portfolio the way enterprise video does (Vimeo + Brightcove); the filings treat it as a standalone transaction/ticketing platform, and it shares the "event/community" adjacency with Meetup (also a Bending Spoons trademark) only loosely — neither filing asserts a formal Eventbrite–Meetup cluster, so none is claimed here.
