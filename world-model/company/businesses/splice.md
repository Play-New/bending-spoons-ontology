## What it is
Splice is a mobile video-editing application, marketed as a powerful smartphone video editor for creators sharing to social media (site-splice, spliceapp.com). Bending Spoons operates it through its subsidiaries Splice Video Editor S.r.l. and AI Creativity S.r.l.; the app page states it is made by AI Creativity S.r.l., under the management of Bending Spoons S.p.A. in Milan (site-splice; bsp-f1). It monetizes as a consumer subscription business (bsp-f1, which discusses Splice subscribers and average revenue per subscriber). Note: this Splice is distinct from the unrelated music-sample platform at splice.com — the Bending Spoons asset is the video editor at spliceapp.com (site-splice).

## What holds the customer
The load-bearing signal here is willingness-to-pay of the committed user versus churn — and the filings state it directly, in the 2024-to-2025 revenue discussion: growth across other businesses was partly offset by a decrease in Splice revenue caused by a decline in the number of subscribers, mitigated by an increase in average revenue per subscriber (bsp-f1 ~L1868; bsp-424b4 ~L1858). That is the classic optimized-asset shape: the user base is contracting, but the users who stay pay more (or are monetized harder), so per-user economics hold up even as the top line for the product softens. Splice is a subscription business (the filings describe it in terms of subscribers and average revenue per subscriber, not MAU or advertising), and the filings do not break out a Splice revenue figure, subscriber count, or NRR (bsp-f1; bsp-424b4). The official pricing page for the video editor could not be retrieved (spliceapp.com/pricing returned 404), so the current tier structure is [to-validate — press only]; the retention/monetization read rests on the filings' revenue commentary (bsp-f1 ~L1868; bsp-424b4 ~L1858).

## Where it sits in the machine
Splice sits at the optimized end of the lifecycle: a mature, held consumer app whose subscriber count is shrinking while revenue-per-subscriber rises, run lean through dedicated Milan subsidiaries (bsp-f1 ~L1868; bsp-424b4 ~L1858). It is a smaller consumer-creator asset rather than part of the enterprise-video cluster; the filings group the 2025 enterprise-video acquisitions (Vimeo, Brightcove) separately and do not cluster Splice with them (bsp-f1 ~L1868; bsp-424b4 ~L1858). Its portfolio role is cash-generative harvest of an installed paying base, not a growth engine.
