## What it is
Vimeo is a video platform that "serves consumers and enterprises with video hosting, management, and streaming solutions" (bsp-f1 ~L394). Its own pre-acquisition filing calls it "the world's most innovative video experience platform, providing a full breadth of video tools through a software-as-a-service (SaaS) model," offering "a turnkey cloud-based solution ... including video hosting and management, intuitive video creation and editing, insightful analytics, artificial intelligence language translations, and enterprise tools" (bsp-f1 ~L5837-5839). Its own site describes it as an ad-free, HD video hosting and player platform bundled with creation and editing tools (screen recorder, in-browser editor, video maker), live streaming and webinars, a collaborative video library, and analytics and monetization options (site-vimeo, https://vimeo.com/features). It runs across two commercial motions: a self-serve subscription business for creators and small teams, and a higher-touch Vimeo Enterprise offering for large organizations (site-vimeo, https://vimeo.com/enterprise). Vimeo's revenue is "derived primarily from fixed SaaS subscription fees paid by customers," with subscription periods ranging from one month to three years and annual subscriptions the most common (bsp-f1 ~L5851-5852).

## The deal
On November 24, 2025, Bending Spoons "completed the acquisition of 100% of the issued and outstanding equity securities of Vimeo, Inc." "for a total consideration of $1.36 billion," accounted for as a business combination (bsp-f1 ~L4990-4996). The F-1 accounts for it as one of only two acquisitions (alongside AOL) deemed "significant" for the periods presented and includes audited Vimeo financials for 2023 and 2024 (bsp-f1 ~L221, ~L4262). Transaction costs (mainly professional fees) were $5 million, recognized in G&A (bsp-f1 ~L4997). The preliminary purchase-price allocation records goodwill of $1,039,478K, a customer base of $169,478K, intellectual properties of $30,930K, other intangible assets of $56,822K, cash and cash equivalents of $317,432K, trade receivables/other current assets of $37,883K, and other non-current assets of $9,415K — total assets acquired $1,661,437K — against liabilities assumed of $302,749K (deferred tax liabilities $71,821K, accrued/other current liabilities $225,595K, other non-current $5,333K), for fair value of net assets acquired of $1,358,688K (bsp-f1 ~L4998). The estimated useful lives are 9 years for the customer base, 5 years for intellectual properties, and 8 years for other intangibles (mainly trademarks) (bsp-f1 ~L4999). The allocation is "preliminary and subject to change," to be finalized within a measurement period not exceeding one year (bsp-f1 ~L5000-5003). In connection with the deal, certain unvested equity awards vested on the change in control; the post-combination portion of $22 million was treated as a separate transaction and recognized as 2025 compensation cost (bsp-f1 ~L5004-5006). The goodwill roll-forward confirms $1.04 billion of 2025 goodwill was attributable to Vimeo (bsp-f1 ~L5073). Vimeo was Bending Spoons' largest single acquisition completed in 2025 by disclosed consideration (Brightcove, the next-largest, was $163M of 2025 revenue and $159M goodwill; AOL's $1.45 billion deal closed January 2, 2026) (bsp-f1 ~L4989, ~L5073, ~L4388).

### Stub-period contribution (post-close, 2025)
Bending Spoons' 2025 consolidated income statement includes only ~5 weeks of Vimeo: "Vimeo's revenue of $43 million and loss before tax of $47 million for the period from the acquisition date (November 24 to December 31, 2025)" (bsp-f1 ~L5007). The loss reflects acquisition-related charges (the $22M equity acceleration and separation costs), not steady-state economics — Vimeo was profitable pre-acquisition (see below). Supplemental pro-forma information (combining Bending Spoons with komoot, Loomly, MileIQ, Harvest, Brightcove, and Vimeo as if acquired January 1, 2024) shows combined revenue of $1,761,916K and net income of $9,143K for 2025 (bsp-f1 ~L5008, ~L5014).

## The playbook applied (where the OFFICIAL record shows it)
The F-1 places Vimeo squarely in the "transform" phase: "much of our current focus is on transforming those more recently acquired, including AOL, Eventbrite, and Vimeo" (bsp-f1 ~L135), and "As we transform AOL, Eventbrite, and Vimeo, and continue to optimize our other businesses, 2026 is shaping up to be strong" (bsp-f1 ~L174). The transformation shows up in the cost structure. For Q1 2026, the F-1 attributes increases in R&D (+116%), sales-and-marketing (+57%), and G&A (+52%) expense partly to "Separation packages offered to team members in connection with the reorganizations of AOL, Eventbrite, and Vimeo" (bsp-f1 ~L1917, ~L1919, ~L1922) — the standard post-acquisition reorganization language — and Q1 2026 reorganization-related expense (a $75.8M non-GAAP add-back for the quarter) "was driven by separation packages offered to team members in connection with the reorganizations of AOL, Brightcove, Eventbrite, Vimeo, and WeTransfer" (bsp-f1 ~L1964, ~L535). For full-year 2025 it also cites "Costs related to the acceleration of equity instruments held by the acquired teams at Brightcove and Vimeo" in R&D, S&M, and G&A (bsp-f1 ~L1883, ~L1887, ~L1891), and quantifies it: 2025 transaction-related expense "included ... $36 million of costs related to the acceleration of equity instruments held by the acquired teams at Brightcove and Vimeo" (bsp-f1 ~L1947) — of which Vimeo's post-combination portion was $22 million (bsp-f1 ~L5006). The exact headcount-reduction percentage is [to-validate — press only] — not stated in either filing. On the product side, Vimeo's own site now foregrounds AI: "smart editing and translation," AI script generation, automatic captions, video transcription, AI translation into "dozens of languages," and AI-generated SEO metadata (site-vimeo, https://vimeo.com/features); Vimeo Enterprise adds deep-search libraries, custom AI language libraries, SSO, SOC II / HIPAA-capable compliance, audit logs, optional DRM, and "starting at 5x storage and 50x bandwidth of other Vimeo plans" (site-vimeo, https://vimeo.com/enterprise). Vimeo's own pre-acquisition description matches the AI framing: "artificial intelligence language translations" are named as a core capability of its "turnkey cloud-based solution" (bsp-f1 ~L5839). ## What holds the customer
The load-bearing signal for Vimeo is willingness-to-pay expressed as committed, largely non-cancellable subscription revenue. "Vimeo's revenue is derived primarily from fixed SaaS subscription fees paid by customers"; subscription periods "generally range from one month to three years with the most common being an annual subscription and are generally non-cancellable" (bsp-f1 ~L5851-5852) — committed users, not casual traffic, are what convert. The scale of that commitment is visible in the balance sheet: deferred revenue of $161,923K at December 31, 2024 ($168,610K at December 31, 2023), and during 2024 Vimeo recognized $165.8 million of revenue that had been in the opening deferred-revenue balance (bsp-f1 ~L5970). The disaggregation in Vimeo's own audited financials shows where that commitment concentrates: for the year ended December 31, 2024, Self-serve & add-ons revenue was $271,691K (down from $285,529K in 2023) while Vimeo enterprise revenue grew to $83,191K (up from $56,499K in 2023), with an "Other" bucket of $62,124K (2024) / $75,186K (2023), on essentially flat total revenue of $417,006K (2024) vs $417,214K (2023) (bsp-f1 ~L5832, ~L5965). By geography, U.S. revenue was $226,790K and all other countries $190,216K in 2024 — the U.S. was the only country above 10% of revenue (bsp-f1 ~L5967, ~L5970). The trend continued into 2025: Vimeo's unaudited nine-month figures show Self-serve $172,410K (down from $175,837K a year earlier) and Vimeo enterprise $74,924K (up from $60,193K), with OTT ($36,392K), Add-ons ($24,804K), and Other ($4,910K), on flat total nine-month revenue of $313,440K vs $313,850K (bsp-f1 ~L6308). The net reading: the self-serve base is shrinking while the higher-commitment enterprise tier is expanding — the retention/willingness-to-pay is migrating upmarket. The F-1 also flags Vimeo as a case where AI-chatbot substitution risk is limited, citing "StreamYard's and Vimeo's video management and streaming offerings" as "sophisticated and relatively niche solutions" (bsp-f1 ~L2851). Note these figures predate the Bending Spoons acquisition; post-acquisition retention under the new pricing and reorganization is [to-validate] — neither filing breaks out Vimeo-specific post-close MAU, paying customers, or churn (the only per-product monthly-active/paying-customer figures given are for WeTransfer and Brightcove, e.g. Brightcove's ~15,000 MAU and ~1,700 monthly paying customers) (bsp-f1 ~L374).

## Pre-acquisition profile (audited standalone, 2023-2024)
Vimeo entered the portfolio as a profitable, cash-generative SaaS business, not a turnaround-by-necessity. Its own audited statements show operating income of $18,683K (2024) and $13,047K (2023), earnings before income taxes of $33,716K (2024) / $24,911K (2023), and net earnings of $27,012K (2024) / $22,032K (2023) — with gross profit of $326,275K on $417,006K revenue in 2024 (a ~78% gross margin) (bsp-f1 ~L5832). It carried $325,276K of cash and $408,797K of total shareholders' equity at December 31, 2024, and generated $56,861K of operating cash flow in 2024 (bsp-f1 ~L5831, ~L5835). Cost structure the machine can compress: 2024 R&D of $109,373K, S&M of $119,869K (down from $151,487K in 2023), G&A of $76,604K, advertising expense of $32.4 million (down from $60.4 million), and stock-based compensation of $32,705K (bsp-f1 ~L5832, ~L5922). The pre-close S&M and advertising pull-back already signals the optimize motion beginning before Bending Spoons even closed.

## Where it sits in the machine
Vimeo is in the transforming lifecycle stage: acquired late November 2025, explicitly named among the businesses currently being transformed, and already showing reorganization/separation costs in the most recent quarter (bsp-f1 ~L135, ~L1964). It is one of the ten main businesses (AOL, Brightcove, Eventbrite, Evernote, Harvest, komoot, Remini, StreamYard, Vimeo, WeTransfer) that together "accounted for more than 80% of our revenue" in Q1 2026 (bsp-f1 ~L375-376). Portfolio clustering in video is supported by the F-1: Bending Spoons also owns Brightcove ("provides video hosting, management, and streaming solutions for enterprises" and "focuses on large enterprises") and StreamYard ("provides video recording and multi-destination live-streaming tools for creators and businesses"), so Vimeo sits inside a video cluster spanning enterprise hosting (Brightcove), creator/consumer hosting and enterprise (Vimeo), and live streaming (StreamYard) (bsp-f1 ~L380, ~L392, ~L374). Two risk flags the F-1 attaches specifically to Vimeo: (1) an April 2026 third-party analytics-vendor security incident that "compromised certain Vimeo customer and user data" (bsp-f1 ~L842); and (2) pending copyright litigation — in March 2021 Sony Music Entertainment Italy, Warner Music Italia, Universal Music Italia, and Warner Music International Services sued Vimeo in the Civil Court of Milan alleging violations of Italian copyright and unfair-competition laws over user-uploaded videos, seeking injunctive relief, damages, and potential penalties of €10,000 per day of delay in removing works; the final claims hearing is scheduled for November 24, 2026, and as of December 31, 2024 and 2025 no accrued legal liabilities were set aside for it (bsp-f1 ~L5046-5057).
