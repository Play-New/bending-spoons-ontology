# The will of Bending Spoons

**Who Bending Spoons is.** Bending Spoons is a Milan technology company that acquires mature digital businesses, transforms and optimizes them, and reinvests the earnings into the next acquisition. The founders' letter frames the strategy as *"outsourcing the search for product-market fit, while directing all of our energy toward excelling at everything else"* (`bsp-f1` ~L84-85) тАФ pouring that energy into being the best possible *operator* of digital businesses. (The evocative "a mix between a private-equity fund and Google" is a press characterization, `[to-validate тАФ press only]`, not the filing's own words.) It is not a portfolio of apps and not a revenue line тАФ it is a **machine** that buys, transforms, and compounds digital businesses; the apps and the numbers are its outputs. It trades on Nasdaq as "BSP", priced at $29.00 per ordinary share at its offering (`bsp-424b4` ~L7); the implied ~$18.4B valuation is `[derived]` (price ├Ч 635,219,310 shares outstanding, `bsp-424b4` ~L1546), not a figure stated in the prospectus.

This file holds its **will** тАФ what it wants and what it refuses. The founders built it from their own experience: a problem they lived, and the strategy they drew from it (`bsp-f1`, Letter from the team, ~L57-88).

## Problem
Building a company by inventing new products is a bet on luck: success hinges on finding product-market fit, and finding it is largely luck (`bsp-f1` ~L71). Luck does not scale and cannot be trained, so you cannot build something large and predictable on top of it. The founders lived this тАФ their first product, Evertale, failed for lack of product-market fit ("essentially no revenue тАж Evertale is a failure", `bsp-f1` ~L66).

## Mission тАФ build the perfect operating machine
Two things follow from the problem: many businesses got lucky on the product but are run suboptimally, so a more skilled operator could improve them (`bsp-f1` ~L74); and operating excellence, unlike luck, is trainable (`bsp-f1` ~L75). So Bending Spoons moves the bet from luck (inventing products) to skill (operating them): become the best possible operators of digital businesses, and "treat the operating machine itself as our most important product" (`bsp-f1` ~L79, ~L87). The better the machine, the more a business gains inside Bending Spoons than as a standalone entity (`bsp-f1` ~L81).

## Intent тАФ compound capital through acquisitions, forever
Operational excellence unlocks efficient growth by acquisition: acquire, transform, reinvest, and keep compounding, outsourcing the search for product-market fit while directing all energy to operating well (`bsp-f1` ~L82, ~L84, ~L85). They expect to allocate almost all available capital to acquisitions for many years; "perhaps our best acquisition opportunity will be the shares of our own company" (`bsp-f1` ~L114-115).

## Constraints тАФ what it refuses
**The deal discipline** (gates `screen` ┬╖ `underwrite` ┬╖ `close`):
- **Never sell a material business** (`bsp-f1` ~L265; the three reasons, ~L2860-2869) тАФ the refusal that separates the model from private equity: never having to sell means underwriting to a long hold, not an exit multiple. *Nuance:* this is **strategy, not law** тАФ the charter expressly permits "monetizing, and disposing ofтАж businesses" (~L3467), and one non-material unit was in fact disposed of in H1 2025, its goodwill impaired (~L5075-5077). The will holds the line; nothing legal does. Wind-downs are the `retire` verb.
- **Hold the IRR hurdles**: 65% levered / 25% unlevered, held even as capital deployed scaled ~10├Ч ($194M in 2023 тЖТ $2.01B in Q1 2026) (`bsp-f1` ~L110-111). *Nuance:* the filing itself says it is "plausible we'll have to lower them" as scale grows (~L112-113) тАФ a bar they expect to move deliberately, not dogma.
- **Margin of safety**: no major investment unless returns stay acceptable "even under severely unfavorable scenarios" (~L2609-2610) тАФ a downside floor distinct from the hurdle.
- **Patience**: decline attractive opportunities when superior ones are likely near-term (~L2611-2612).
- **No bid escalation**: a compelling offer upfront, "generally unwilling to increase it significantly thereafter" (~L2613-2616) тАФ reputation as pricing discipline.
- **Assumptions before simulations**: the underwriting model is not run until the assumptions are frozen (~L2601-2602) тАФ explicit anti-bias sequencing.

**The org discipline** (gates `talent` ┬╖ `transform`):
- **Keep the org flat**: no more than three managerial layers from CEO to individual contributor (`bsp-f1` ~L2519).
- **Centralized talent authority**: the Talent team holds final authority over all hiring *and* separation decisions (~L2476); **proactive separation** тАФ part ways with adequate performers when stronger people are available (~L2479-2481).
- **Equity is acquired, not granted**: Spooners obtain equity via a cash trade-off, never by automatic grant тАФ explicit dilution discipline (~L2494-2497).

**The capital discipline** (gates `reinvest` / capital allocation):
- **Favor returns over organic growth** (`bsp-f1` ~L162) тАФ and **optimize the whole portfolio**, "even when that requires deprioritizing individual businesses" (~L2501-2504); run as a single segment, "optimizing overall portfolio performance rather than managing individual businesses as independent profit centers" (~L4582-4583).
- **No dividends тАФ reinvest everything**: retain all funds; "almost all available capital to acquisitions for many years" (~L513-516, ~L114).
- **Give-back**: a low single-digit percentage of net income to donations, expected to continue (~L2002-2005).

**Founder control** (what makes the rest holdable):
- Dual-class: **class A shares carry 5 votes each** vs 1 per ordinary share (`bsp-f1` ~L1389, bylaws ~L3510); post-IPO the four class-A founders hold **82.71% of total voting power** (`bsp-424b4` ~L13). Control is **personal and non-transferable**: class A converts to ordinary on transfer outside the class-A circle, on death, or if a holder falls below **37,500,000 class A shares** (~L3511-3519, ~L3514); board slates may be submitted only by the board or тЙе5% holders (~L3554-3569). As a foreign private issuer it also reports less than a domestic filer (~L40). This is what lets it hold the buy-and-hold horizon against market pressure to sell. тЖТ the `founders` node.

**External covenants** (the banks' constraints тАФ binding, but not chosen; they gate the same verbs from outside):
- **Leverage ratio тЙд 4.00** (net debt / adjusted EBITDA), kept at 2.24 (FY2025) and 2.19 (Q1 2026) (~L1751-1753, ~L4541-4546).
- **Negative covenants** restricting asset disposals, distributions, additional debt, certain acquisitions, mergers, and reorganizations; breach тЖТ immediate repayment (~L4547-4549).
- The board's delegated capital-increase authority may **not** be exercised if it would cause a change of control (Nasdaq Rule 5635(b)) (~L3481-3488) тАФ a hard ceiling on dilution that protects the founder-control constraint itself.
